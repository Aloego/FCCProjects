Thomas Sankara

A bold and transformative force that challenged neo-colonialism
promoted self-reliance, gender equality, and environmental sustainability.
Inspired global movements for justice and independence.

His Timeline

1949 – Birth & Early Life

December 21, 1949 – Born in Yako, Upper Volta (now Burkina Faso). Raised in a modest family, he excelled academically and joined the military.

1966 – 1982: Military & Political Awakening

1966 – Witnessed the military overthrow of Upper Volta’s first president, shaping his political views..

1970 – Trained in Madagascar, where he was exposed to leftist ideologies and saw socialist reforms in action.

1976 – Became commander of the Commando Training Center in Pô, where he trained soldiers in revolutionary ideas.

1981 – Appointed Secretary of State for Information but resigned after refusing to serve a corrupt government.

1982 – Became Prime Minister of Upper Volta under Jean-Baptiste Ouédraogo.

April 1983 – Sankara was arrested for his radical ideas and anti-imperialist stance, sparking public protests.

1983 – The Revolution & Birth of Burkina Faso

August 4, 1983 – Led a military coup supported by young revolutionaries, becoming President of Upper Volta at age 33.

1984 – Renamed the country Burkina Faso ("Land of Upright People") to erase its colonial name.

1983 – 1987: His Fight Against Neo-Colonialism & Western Influence

1️. Economic Independence & Anti-Imperialism

✔ Rejected foreign aid & IMF/World Bank loans, calling them tools of enslavement.

✔ Encouraged self-sufficiency in food & clothing, making Burkina Faso a model for agricultural success.

✔ Nationalized land & redistributed wealth to the people.

2️. Women’s Liberation & Social Reforms

✔ First African leader to actively promote women’s rights by banning forced marriages, polygamy, and FGM.

✔ Encouraged women in government & military, setting an example for gender equality.

✔ promoted free education & healthcare, launching a vaccination campaign that saved millions of children.

3️. Environmental Protection

✔ Planted 10 million trees to stop desertification, making him an early environmental activist.

✔ Fought against Western companies exploiting Africa’s resources.

4️. African Unity & Anti-Debt Movement

✔ Called for African nations to unite and reject Western debt, stating: “If we all refuse to pay, they can do nothing to us!”

✔ Inspired anti-colonial movements worldwide, influencing leaders like Jerry Rawlings (Ghana) and Muammar Gaddafi (Libya).

✔ Criticized France's continued control over West African economies, angering Western powers.

1987 – Assassination & Betrayal

October 15, 1987 – Sankara was assassinated in a coup led by Blaise Compaoré, his former ally, with alleged backing from France.

His body was secretly buried, and his policies were reversed by Compaoré, who ruled for 27 years.

Legacy (1987 – Present)

2015 – Compaoré was overthrown, leading to calls for Sankara’s justice.

2022 – Blaise Compaoré was convicted for his role in Sankara’s assassination.

Today – Sankara’s ideas on self-reliance, anti-imperialism, gender equality, and African unity continue to inspire global movements.

Sankara’s spirit lives on as a symbol of African dignity, resistance, and true independence!

Africa needs more leaders like Thomas Sankara, who put the people first and rejected corruption. His legacy remains a beacon of hope for the oppressed.
- Nelson Mandela
